Carbon Dioxide Trends Around the World

Global Trends


Carbon dioxide is a potent greenhouse gas that is produced when fossil fuels are burnt. Carbon dioxide contributes to the greenhouse effect which leads to global warming and climate change

Significant increases in global emissions began around the end of World War II when global travel, energy consumption and agriculture dramatically increased.

Since 1990, the world has produced 116.13 billion tonnes of CO2 has been released into the atmosphere.

Most countries produced between 0 and 10 tonnes per capita with only a handfull of countries with emissions higher than 15 tonnes per capita. The highest CO2 per capita was recoded in 2005 in Qatar at 47.7 tonnnes per capita


Continental Trends


Europe and North America were the main contributors of carbon dioxide emissions in the 1900s making up at least 50% combined.

However, since 2000, the main contributor has become Asia which made up 60% of emsisions in 2020 .

This may be do to the increased economic growth that Asian countries experienced throughout the 2000's and the increased demand for fossil fuels as Asian populations grew


The plot above highlights a positive correlation between GDP per capita and CO2 per capita indicating that wealthier countries are higher emitters.

Conversely, there appears to be little to no correlation between Landmass and CO2 per capita. This is mainly due to the normalisation by population that the data has gone through.

Asian countries such as Qatar and Kuwait have extremely high per capita emissions due to large scale oil refinement processes and a small populations.
Similarly, extremely small countries such as Luxenburg and Trinidad and Tabago have a high emission per capita due to a small population and emissions that may come from transport.
